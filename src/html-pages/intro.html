Introduction To Hierarchical Flow Modelling

The purpose of this site is to provide a simple and straight forward means of preparing, accessing and presenting information about systems. These can be any sort of system whether natural, technological, chemical or conceptual.

The basic approach consists of tracking flows (whether of data, chemicals or energy) and their transformations through various physical or conceptual devices or systems.

Consider for example the flow of petrol to power a car, we might represent this as:

Petrol Station -->-(flow:Petrol)-- Car -->-(flow:Exhaust)-- Atmosphere

In this case the component nodes of the flow are Petrol Station, Car and Atmosphere and the flows are Petrol and Exhaust.

Because this is a hierarchical modelling system, we can provide more details of say, the Car, ie:

(flow:Petrol)-->--Cylinder-->-(flow:Motion)-- Piston -->-- Drive Assembly

The Flow Modelling page provides both for the diagramming of such flows and their hierarchical further definitions.

Facilities are also provided for adding graphics and web links for further information to the diagrams.

In addition the diagrams can be imported or exported for external creation or editing in a json format, which also makes it suitable for use in other applications.
